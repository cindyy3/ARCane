Society's value of art is decreasing. We're here to help.





What is happening?

More and more people are losing interest in art and choosing to invest in other industries. This in turn leads to cuts in funding for arts programs in schools, leaving students unable to partipate in or explore the arts, and creating an overall societal lack of interest for the arts.

"To create a work of art is to create the world."

Wassily Kandinsky

How do we know?

The National Assembly of State Arts Agencies (NASAA) is a non-profit organization located in Washington D.C. that is an association of the United State's 56 state and jurisdictional arts agencies. The NASAA publishes a revenue report twice a year which provides information on financial support for the arts and places individual State Art Agencies' funding within the context of national trends.

According to the State Arts Agency Revenue Report of the Fiscal Year 2021...

  • Total appropriations to state arts agencies decreased by 17.9%, $87.5 million, between fiscal years 2020 and 2021.

  • $25.1 million of total legislative appropriations came from line items that pass through SAA budgets. Excluding line items, which declined significantly, appropriations for funds that state arts agencies control decreased by 2.9% from FY2020 to FY2021.

  • Twenty-four SAAs reported decreases, with a median decrease of 12.3%.

  • Total per capita appropriations to SAAs decreased by $0.26 in FY2021, bringing them to a total of $1.21 per person.

  • When adjusting for inflation, total legislative appropriations remain 38.7% below FY2001 levels.

Why is art important?

IPaintMyMind is an "organization built on the notion that art can change lives and that access to moments that inspire us are something every person and community deserves." They believe art "dares us to build new creative solutions regardless of our tools" and "connect artists, businesses, and local communities to bring value to everyone."

According to the information from IPaintMyMind, there are four studies that state...

  • Students With Access To Arts Education Are 5x Less Likely To Drop Out Of School

  • 4x More Likely To Be Recognized For Academic Achievement

  • 3x More Likely To Pursue A Bachelor’s Degree

  • 30% More Likely To Pursue A Professional Career.

  • Low-Income Students WIth Access To Arts Education Are 2x As Likely To Graduate From College Than Their Peers With No Arts Education

Why is this happening?

The decreasing funding and slow disappearance of art courses in high schools is recognized by many organizations in states across the United States. For instance, Pepperdine University and State Impact (Oklahoma) by the National Public Radio (NPR) published articles on the decline of funding for the arts. Pepperdine University is a private research university located in California. The NPR is a nonprofit media organization headquartered in Washington D.C.

According to the Pepperdine University and the NPR...

  • It is challenging to prove that an arts education is needed, as there aren’t standardized tests that can quantify its value.

  • Funding for the arts is complex. In the U.S., the art industry is not controlled by a single person or agency. Instead, a combination of federal, state, regional and local agencies provide financing for the arts.

  • One big hurdle in maintaining these programs is the difficulty of finding certified arts teachers, especially in rural districts.

What now?

Due to cuts in governmental funding, the role of individuals have become greatly significant in helping students receive art education. For example, public schools collect fees from students that participate in the arts. A common example is band and orchestra. Outside of school, there are organizations that work to provide students opportunities to engage in art. Try looking through some of these organizations and engage!
